Minimum Variance Unbiased Estimator (MVUE)

1. Definition

Let X=(X1,…,Xn) be a random sample from a family {f(x;θ)∣θ∈Θ}.

A statistic T(X) is the MVUE of a target g(θ) if both of the following hold:

  1. Unbiasedness
    𝔼θ[T(X)]=g(θ) for every θ∈Θ.
  2. Minimum variance
    Among all unbiased estimators of g(θ), T(X) has the smallest variance:
    Varθ[T(X)]≤Varθ[T~(X)] for any other unbiased T~(X).

2. Existence and Uniqueness

If an MVUE exists, it is unique. Suppose T1 and T2 are both MVUEs for g(θ). Then 𝔼θ[T1−T2]=0 and Varθ[T1−T2]=0, which implies T1=T2 almost surely.

3. Finding the MVUE (Lehmann-Scheffe)

The Lehmann-Scheffe theorem provides a constructive recipe:

  1. Find a sufficient statistic S(X) using, for example, the factorization theorem.
  2. Check completeness of S(X). If S(X) is complete and sufficient, then for any unbiased estimator U(X) of g(θ), the conditional expectation
    T(X)=𝔼[U(X)∣S(X)]
    is the unique MVUE of g(θ).

4. Examples

Example 1: Mean of a Normal Distribution

Let X1,…,Xn~𝒩(μ,σ2) with σ2 known. The sample mean X¯=1n∑iXi is unbiased for μ and satisfies Var(X¯)=σ2/n. No other unbiased estimator has smaller variance, so X¯ is the MVUE of μ.

Example 2: Variance of a Normal Distribution

Let X1,…,Xn~𝒩(μ,σ2).

  • If μ is known, then S2=1n∑i(Xi−μ)2 is unbiased for σ2 and is the MVUE.
  • If μ is unknown, the adjusted estimator σ^2=1n−1∑i(Xi−X¯)2 remains unbiased and is still the MVUE of σ2.

5. Connection to the Cramer-Rao Bound

For an unbiased estimator T(X) of g(θ),

Varθ[T(X)]≥(g′(θ))2I(θ),
where I(θ) is the Fisher information. If equality holds, T(X) is efficient, meaning it achieves the Cramer-Rao lower bound and is therefore the MVUE.

6. Summary

Property Meaning
Unbiased 𝔼[T]=g(θ)
Minimum variance No other unbiased estimator has smaller variance
Unique At most one MVUE exists for a given g(θ)
Constructive tool Lehmann-Scheffe via complete sufficient statistics
Relation to CRLB Efficient estimators hit the Cramer-Rao bound
